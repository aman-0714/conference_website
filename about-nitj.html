About the Institute

Dr. B. R. Ambedkar National Institute of Technology Jalandhar is among the 31 NITs established by the Ministry of Education (earlier MHRD), Government of India. The Institute came into existence in 1987 (earlier known as Regional Engineering College, Jalandhar) and was conferred the status of "Institute of National Importance" by an Act of Parliament in 2007.

The institute offers B.Tech., M.Sc., M.Tech., and Ph.D. programmes across a wide range of disciplines, including Biotechnology, Chemical Engineering, Civil Engineering, Computer Science and Engineering, Electrical Engineering, Electronics and Communication Engineering, Industrial and Production Engineering, Information Technology, Instrumentation and Control Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, and Textile Technology.

Campus & Surroundings

The campus sits on a sprawling, green stretch of land along the Jalandhar–Amritsar Highway in Punjab, offering a calm, well-connected setting for conference attendees. Jalandhar itself is centrally located in Punjab, with convenient road and rail links to Amritsar, Chandigarh, and Delhi, making the campus easy to reach for both domestic and international visitors.

The institute provides a vibrant academic environment that fosters innovation, critical thinking, and interdisciplinary research, supported by state-of-the-art infrastructure and modern laboratories — many of which conference attendees will get to experience firsthand during IIPESS-2027.

Research & Innovation Focus

With a strong focus on research, innovation, and entrepreneurship, NIT Jalandhar actively contributes to national missions in energy, sustainability, digital transformation, and advanced manufacturing — themes that closely align with the focus areas of IIPESS-2027.
